Timeline of Indian Heritage

2600 BCE

The Indus Valley Civilization

Explore the ancient cities of Mohenjo-Daro and Harappa, marvels of urban planning that flourished along the Indus River. These cities featured advanced drainage systems, granaries, and the mysterious undeciphered script.

Read Story
250 BCE

Sanchi Stupa - Ashoka's Legacy

Commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE, the Great Stupa at Sanchi is one of the oldest stone structures in India. Its intricate carvings depict scenes from the life of Buddha and Jataka tales.

Read Story
1300 CE

Hampi - The Forgotten Empire

Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, Hampi's ruins spread over 4,100 hectares. The Vitthala Temple with its musical pillars and the majestic stone chariot stand as testaments to its glorious past.

Read Story
1653 CE

Taj Mahal - Monument of Love

Built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, this ivory-white marble mausoleum took 22 years and 20,000 artisans to complete. Its perfect symmetry and intricate pietra dura work remain unmatched.

Read Story
1947 CE

India Gate - Memorial of Sacrifice

Standing tall in the heart of New Delhi, this 42-meter high arch commemorates the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army in World War I. Designed by Edwin Lutyens, it's a symbol of national pride.
